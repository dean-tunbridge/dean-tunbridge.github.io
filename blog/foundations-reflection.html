Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think that programming is an incredibly hard skill to learn, and often times it will be frustrating. You you will be working as part of a team, so it is essential to be able to keep track of how you are feeling, your emotions, and the feelings of everyone you're working with.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not at all. Obviously the main reason I'm here is to learn how to become a developer, but any way in which I can improve myself as a human being, or any skills that help me develop emotionally, or give me more confidence is always welcome. Also we have enough time in foundations to learn the amount of programming we are expected to, as well as the human skills. I don't feel like I'm far away from being where I would hope to be in terms of knowledge of web development, as well as learning something new about a brand new subject in human skills.

As mentioned in the resources, companies look for people with high EQ when hiring. It is as important, if not more important than IQ, so I feel like the human skills are an essential piece of the puzzle.
