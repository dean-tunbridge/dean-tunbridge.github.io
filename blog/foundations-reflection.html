Human Skills Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Having shared values as a team can help guide ethical decision-making, and it is especially important in the IT field as you are often dealing with personal information, or building things that are designed to help people. It is also a lot easier to make decisions be they big or small as a team if you are aligned in your goals.

Empathy is important and not just in a programming and learning sense. When working as a team it is essential to be able to consider everyone's feelings before making decisions. Often it seems people find it hard/or just choose not to try and see things from another persons perspective.

Self awareness is another essential skill. You need to be able to realise how you are being perceived by others to ensure you can work competently in a team. If you are able to recognise your own strengths and weaknesses you can more effectively make changes and improvements to them which will benefit everyone.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Learning about neuroplasticity was really interesting and surprising to me. I had no idea how highly EQ was valued by employers. Now I know this it does make a lot of sense but I hadn't even considered it. I think my previous industry which was hospitality could learn a thing or two about EQ!

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Honesty it could well be writing this blog post. I really don't know how to answer most of the questions. Which probably suggests I have trouble getting my thoughts about ego, identity, and values down on paper.
Also after writing the learning plan, I have struggled keeping to it. I haven't been going to the gym like I promised myself I would, and I haven't been reflecting as much as I'd hoped. There is always room for improvement so hopefully I can develop these habits in the coming weeks.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think that programming is an incredibly hard skill to learn, and often times it will be frustrating. You you will be working as part of a team, so it is essential to be able to keep track of how you are feeling, your emotions, and the feelings of everyone you're working with.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not at all. Obviously the main reason I'm here is to learn how to become a developer, but any way in which I can improve myself as a human being, or any skills that help me develop emotionally, or give me more confidence is always welcome. Also we have enough time in foundations to learn the amount of programming we are expected to, as well as the human skills. I don't feel like I'm far away from being where I would hope to be in terms of knowledge of web development, as well as learning something new about a brand new subject in human skills.

As mentioned in the resources, companies look for people with high EQ when hiring. It is as important, if not more important than IQ, so I feel like the human skills are an essential piece of the puzzle.
