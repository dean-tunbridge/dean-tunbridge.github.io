Neuroplasticity

Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Neuroplasticity is the brains ability to change and adapt due to experience. Neuro relates to the neurons of the brain, whilst plasticity relates to the brains ability to change, or be malleable.
Some of the benefits of improved neuroplasticity include; the ability to learn new things, improved neural pathways, the ability to improve existing knowledge and capabilities, enhance existing cognitive capabilities, reigniting or improving areas of cognitive function that have been lost or declined through a number of factors such as old age, or brain injuries, and even aiding in the recovery from strokes and traumatic brain injuries.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

Firstly, the more I learn about this subject, the more fascinating I find it and the more I want to implement it into my study. So to engage with the principles of neuroplasticity, I will do the following:

Practice mindfulness: I have been trying to start my day with a short meditation, but I am finding that it's proving difficult in the Morning as I'm being attacked by two hungry cats as soon as I wake up. So from now on I will try one guided meditation in the evening before bed.

Get plenty of rest: With my previous career, getting 5 or 6 hours of sleep per night was the norm. This had so many negative effects on my life including mood, cognitive abilities, and alertness. Now I go to bed by 10pm and get up at 7am, and the difference is night and day. I feel more alert at all times, which means my caffeine consumption has halved. This has had a knock on effect of reducing jitteriness and improving focus. I feel like I also look healthier and my mod is much more stable.

Exercise: Over the last decade or so I have been on and off with exercising regularly. Even earlier this year I was in the gym 5 days a week. Due to an injury at work I stopped and haven't returned even though I am fully fit again. The biggest improvements in my health and wellbeing ever, are the times when I have worked out consistently. I know what I need to do, it's just a case of finding that motivation again.

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

Growth mindset is the idea that students/Individuals who believe their skills can be improved through hard work, help from others, and using good strategies can achieve more than people with a fixed mindset (those who believe their talents are innate gifts). People with a growth mindset worry less about looking smart and put more energy into learning. Companies who have transitioned to a growth mindset report the following results based on a study published by Harvard Business Review

Employees in a “growth mindset” company are:

47% likelier to say that their colleagues are trustworthy

34% likelier to feel a strong sense of ownership and commitment to the company

65% likelier to say that the company supports risk taking

49% likelier to say that the company fosters innovation.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Absolutely. As with neuroplasticity, the more I learn, the more I take interest in the subject. I am guilty of having a fixed mindset, not to suggest I think i'm talented, it's more the feeling of a lack of innate skill and talent that has caused a fixed mindset.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I have recently turned 36 years old, and it's very easy to fall into the trap of believing you've reached your ceiling, or don't have the ability to learn new things as easy as you used to (I've been playing guitar for 20 years and still suck!). This idea has given me a lot of encouragement, as I know I will never be the most intelligent or naturally talented programmer, but I feel like now, with hard work I can certainly achieve what I want to within the industry.
