Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te whare tapa whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing (taha tinana (physical wellbeing), taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing), taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing), taha whānau (family wellbeing)).
It was developed by Sir Mason Durie to provide a Māori perspective on health. It is based around the wharenui (meeting house), with the wharenui’s connection with the whenua (land) forming the foundation for the other dimensions. By strengthening all 5 walls or dimensions, you support your health and wellbeing, as well as the health and wellbeing of your whānau. Likewise, if one of the other walls or dimensions has become weakened, you can use the support of the other walls to strengthen the weakened wall.

Below I have created a plan using Te Whare Tapa Whā model to help me in my study and in my general wellbeing.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

As the foundations study has gone on, I have felt my motivation gradually increasing. I am going to hold myself accountable to go to the gym 5 days a week without fail. I have added an alarm on my phone that will go off at 9pm every evening, and no matter what I am doing or how I am feeling, I will walk to the gym and exercise for at least 30 minutes to an hour. I have built a workout plan after extensive research that should cover all the bases. I have also limited my alchohol consumption to the weekends.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

Unfortunately, I don't have any family members in New Zealand as they all live in the UK which makes it hard sometimes. I speak to my Dad almost every day via messaging apps, as well as my siblings and some of my friends from back home. I do have support from my partner, and we always go out to eat at least once per week, as well as cooking nice meals at home. I also have 2 pet cats and I absolutely consider them part of my family.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

As I have outlined in other blog posts, I have tried to do guided meditations in the morning, but the cat members of my family soon put that idea to bed. I have now adjusted the plan and I try doing them in the evening before bed as I have more free time then. I think the exercise routine I mentioned above, as well as having a good healthy diet will also benefit me.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

The older I get, and the more time I have spent reading some my favourite subjects (history, physics, consciousness, and astronomy for example) the more open I am to believe in a higher power. That is not to say I believe in any particular religion, but I do spend a decent amount of time each day thinking about it. This is probably the main wall or dimension of the wharenui (meeting house) that I am already quite happy with. I think if I continue to do what I have been doing, and combine it with the improvements I hope to make in other aspects of Te Whare Tapa Whā, my overall wellbeing will improve massively.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

One of my favourite things to do is to go for bush walks. We are so lucky in New Zealand to have such beautiful surroundings. I live in Mission Bay, so I have many walks within 5-10 minutes of my house. Obviously it's harder to do in the winter, but I do try and get out of the house and go for a walk every day. walking down to the beach and looking over to Rangitoto Island has been a special moment for me many times since I moved over here.
